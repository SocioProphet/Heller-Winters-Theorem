## A-HW-PRIME-001 — Named number-theoretic objects, not physical constants

The prime-circle construction (`HW-PRIME-CIRCLE-001`) produces named objects of analytic number theory: the primorial totient sequence, the Jacobsthal function at primorials, and wheel-gap / twin-prime-adjacency diagnostics.

**Failure mode.** Citing `HW-PRIME-CIRCLE-001` to claim that the prime-circle construction derives a physical constant, including the fine-structure constant or any other dimensionless physical coupling.

**Correct statement.** No computation in `HW-PRIME-CIRCLE-001` yields a physical constant. The note identifies number-theoretic structure. Any future connection between prime-circle objects and physical constants is an open question requiring a separate identifier, claim grade, derivation, and explicit accounting of free parameters.

**Closure condition.** None. This discipline applies permanently to `HW-PRIME-CIRCLE-001`. A future derivation, if one is produced, must be its own claim and must not retroactively change the grade of this identified-correspondence note.

## A-HW-PRIME-002 — Finite wheel verification is not asymptotic prime theory

Finite verification of primorial wheel residues, cyclic gaps, and distinct-gap counts does not prove an asymptotic theorem about primes, prime gaps, Jacobsthal growth, or zeta zeros.

**Failure mode.** Citing the first nine checked primorial wheels as evidence for an asymptotic growth law or as progress on RH / GRH.

**Correct statement.** The finite computations in `HW-PRIME-CIRCLE-001` identify exact finite wheel objects and verify named-sequence correspondence over the checked range. Growth laws remain separate theorem or conjecture surfaces.

**Closure condition.** None. Any asymptotic result requires a separate theorem-grade identifier.
